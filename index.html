MedLearn AI

AI-Powered Healthcare Learning & Credentialing Platform

"Education can be revolutionized with AI — when used right, it doesn't replace teachers, it amplifies their impact to reach every learner."

View Live Demo HealthStream Pitch Deck

Motivation

Healthcare training is stuck in the past. Organizations spend millions on generic training modules that don't reflect their specific protocols. When clinical guidelines update, someone has to manually revise hundreds of modules. Josh Bersin's research shows 74% of companies can't keep up with skills demand — and healthcare is no exception.

I wanted to explore what happens when AI generates training content dynamically from YOUR hospital's actual protocols. What if nurses could learn from their own institution's procedures, not generic textbooks? What if compliance tracking happened automatically instead of in spreadsheets?

This project was built with HealthStream in mind — a company that serves 5+ million healthcare professionals and could benefit immensely from AI-powered content generation and adaptive learning systems.

Problem

Healthcare organizations face a content creation crisis. Training takes 40+ hours per module to develop. Generic content doesn't match hospital-specific protocols. Credentialing lives in spreadsheets with unknown compliance scores until audits. When OSHA updates bloodborne pathogen rules or AHA revises ACLS algorithms, the entire training library needs manual updates.

The Credentialing Challenge: Healthcare facilities must track certifications, licenses, and competencies for every staff member. NCQA requires 10 specific credential verifications, while CMS mandates 5 additional compliance checks. Most organizations discover gaps only during audits — by then, it's too late.

Document Upload

Upload hospital protocols and AI learns from them instantly for personalized training.

Credentialing & Compliance

NCQA Compliance Tracking: The platform monitors all 10 NCQA credentialing requirements including license verification, DEA certificates, malpractice history, board certifications, education verification, work history, sanctions checks, and hospital privilege verification.

CMS Requirements: Automated tracking for Medicare/Medicaid enrollment status, exclusion list monitoring (OIG/SAM), National Provider Identifier verification, and state-specific licensure requirements.

Real-Time Alerts: The system sends proactive notifications 90, 60, and 30 days before any credential expires. Dashboard displays organization-wide compliance scores with drill-down capabilities to identify at-risk staff members.

Challenges

Building a Dual-RAG architecture that balances internal hospital documents with external PubMed research was technically complex. Ensuring question quality matched NCLEX standards required extensive prompt engineering and validation loops with nursing education experts.

Implementing real-time compliance calculations for NCQA (10 checks) and CMS (5 requirements) while keeping the UI responsive was challenging. The credentialing logic required careful handling of date calculations, grace periods, and state-specific requirements.

Deploying on free-tier hosting with memory constraints required careful optimization of model loading, caching strategies, and lazy loading of features to stay within 512MB RAM limits.

Impact & ROI

For Healthcare Organizations: Potential 60-70% reduction in content creation time. Automatic compliance tracking eliminates audit surprises. Personalized learning paths improve knowledge retention by 40%. Real-time protocol updates mean training is always current.

ROI Calculation: If a 500-bed hospital spends $125K/year on training development and this reduces it by 60%, that's $75K annual savings. Add avoided compliance penalties (average $50K per violation) and improved patient outcomes from better-trained staff.

HealthStream Alignment: This platform directly addresses HealthStream's mission to improve healthcare workforce development. It could serve as a next-generation module for their existing Learning Center platform, reaching their 5+ million healthcare professional user base.

ClearLab AI

Health Plan Design & Simulation Platform

"Smart healthcare decisions start with understanding the data — simulate before you implement."

Motivation

Health plan design is often based on guesswork. Employers set copays and deductibles without understanding how these decisions affect employee behavior, healthcare utilization, and total costs. What if we could simulate thousands of scenarios before committing to a plan design?

What if AI could predict which members will skip care due to high copays, or which policies will cause network leakage? ClearLab AI brings data-driven decision making to health benefits design, inspired by the myCigna experience but focused on empowering benefits teams with predictive analytics.

Problem

Health benefits teams make million-dollar decisions with limited visibility. They don't know how a $10 copay increase affects ER utilization. They can't predict which members will switch to out-of-network providers. Policy changes often have unintended consequences discovered only after implementation.

The myCigna member experience has friction points that cause abandoned care journeys, but there's no systematic way to identify and fix them. Benefits managers need a sandbox to experiment before committing to changes that affect thousands of employees.

Key Features — The 5 Rooms

Room 1: Plan Design Studio

Interactive copay sliders with real-time Monte Carlo simulations predicting utilization, costs, and skip-care risk.

Room 2: Member Journey Lab

Sankey diagrams and friction heatmaps showing where members abandon care journeys. K-means clustering identifies friction archetypes.

Room 3: Policy Impact Chamber

Before/after policy comparisons with fairness audits, disparate impact analysis, and auto-generated policy memos.

Room 4: Scenario Bridge

Cross-room execution with unified results dashboard, scenario versioning, and side-by-side comparison views.

Room 5: ClearBot

Natural language interface powered by Gemini — "Make this plan 10% cheaper and rerun simulation."

Simulation Engine

Monte Carlo modeling with behavioral economics: price elasticity curves, income-adjusted sensitivity, condition-based utilization.

Challenges

Building realistic synthetic data that captures the complexity of healthcare utilization patterns was the first hurdle — 10K member profiles with age, conditions, income brackets, and correlated claim patterns.

Implementing Monte Carlo simulations with behavioral economics logic (price elasticity curves, income-adjusted sensitivity, condition-based utilization) required extensive research into healthcare economics. Creating an intuitive UI that makes complex simulations accessible to non-technical benefits managers was crucial.

Integrating ClearBot to execute real actions (not just answer questions) pushed the boundaries of LLM agent design — it needed to understand plan configurations, trigger simulations, and modify scenarios through natural language.

Impact & ROI

For Employers: Simulate plan designs before committing millions. Identify which copay changes will cause skip-care behavior. Predict network leakage and adjust tier structures accordingly. Run fairness audits to ensure equitable access across demographics.

ROI Calculation: A mid-size employer (500 employees) spending $5M/year on health benefits could save 5-10% ($250K-$500K) by optimizing plan design. Add reduced administrative costs from better UX, lower employee turnover from better benefits, and avoided compliance penalties from fairness audits.

ReviewGuard

Enhancing Deficient Peer Review Detection via LLM-Driven Data Augmentation

"AI can help maintain the quality standards that keep scientific progress honest and rigorous."

Read Paper View on arXiv

Motivation

Peer review serves as the gatekeeper of science, yet the surge in conference submissions and widespread adoption of LLMs in scholarly evaluation present unprecedented challenges. While recent work has focused on using LLMs to improve review efficiency, unchecked deficient reviews from both human experts and AI systems threaten to systematically undermine academic integrity.

We asked: can we automatically detect and categorize deficient reviews before they damage the scientific process? Can we build tools that help program chairs identify low-quality reviews and maintain the integrity of peer review?

Problem

Top AI conferences like ICLR and NeurIPS receive thousands of submissions. Reviewers are overwhelmed, leading to hasty, superficial, or biased reviews. Some reviews lack substantive feedback. Others show clear signs of not reading the paper carefully.

With LLMs now being used to generate reviews, the problem may accelerate. There's no systematic way to flag deficient reviews before they influence acceptance decisions. Program chairs need automated tools to maintain review quality at scale.

Key Contributions

Large-Scale Dataset

6,634 papers with 24,657 real reviews and 46,438 synthetic reviews from ICLR and NeurIPS via OpenReview.

LLM-Driven Augmentation

GPT-4.1 annotation with human validation for training data generation at scale.

Feature Analysis

Deficient reviews show lower ratings, higher self-reported confidence, reduced structural complexity, and more negative sentiment.

Fine-Tuned Models

Encoder-based models and open-source LLMs trained specifically for deficient review detection.

Four-Stage Framework

Data collection → GPT-4.1 annotation → Synthetic augmentation → Model fine-tuning pipeline.

Deficiency Categories

Systematic taxonomy of deficient review types for categorization and intervention.

Challenges

Defining "deficient" in a way that's consistent and actionable required careful taxonomy development. We needed to balance catching genuinely problematic reviews while avoiding false positives that would burden good reviewers.

Collecting and processing reviews from OpenReview at scale involved significant data engineering. Ensuring GPT-4.1 annotations were accurate required human validation loops and iterative refinement of prompts. The class imbalance between sufficient and deficient reviews required careful handling during model training.

Impact

For Academic Conferences: Automated quality control for peer review. Early flagging of deficient reviews before they influence decisions. Data-driven insights into reviewer behavior patterns. Foundation for improving the entire peer review ecosystem.

Broader Impact: As AI-generated content floods academia, tools like ReviewGuard become essential for maintaining scientific integrity. This research contributes to the growing field of AI safety in academic contexts.
